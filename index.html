About the LSPD

Serving Los Santos since 1894.

Mission

Who we are

The Los Santos Police Department is the primary municipal law enforcement agency for the City of Los Santos, responsible for the prevention of crime, protection of life and property, and preservation of public order.

Founded in 1894, the LSPD has grown from a small constabulary of 40 officers to one of the largest municipal police departments in the state of San Andreas. The department operates nine geographic area divisions, four major operational bureaus, and over eighteen specialized units serving a metropolitan population of more than 3.2 million residents.

The LSPD operates under the authority of the City of Los Santos and the San Andreas Department of Public Safety, alongside partner agencies including the Los Santos County Sheriff's Department and the San Andreas Highway Patrol.

Bureaus & Units

Four major operational bureaus and eighteen specialized units.

Major bureaus

Operational bureaus

Bureau I

Patrol services bureau

Oversees all nine area divisions and uniformed patrol operations. Primary first-response capability for all calls for service across Los Santos.

Bureau II

Detective bureau

Coordinates all investigative units including Homicide, Robbery-Homicide, Burglary, Auto Theft, and Financial Crimes across the department.

Bureau III

Special operations bureau

Commands SWAT, Air Support, K-9, Gang and Narcotics, Vice, and all other specialized enforcement units requiring advanced training and equipment.

Bureau IV

Administrative bureau

Manages personnel, training, internal affairs, records, communications, and departmental logistics and budget administration.

Detective bureau

Investigative units

Homicide

Homicide division

Investigates all homicides, officer-involved shootings, and suspicious deaths within city jurisdiction. 24-hour callout rotation.

RHD

Robbery-homicide division

Handles high-profile robberies, kidnapping, extortion, and cases with jurisdictional overlap or significant public interest.

Financial crimes

Financial crimes unit

Investigates fraud, money laundering, embezzlement, and organized financial crime including racketeer influenced operations.

Burglary / theft

Property crimes unit

Investigates residential and commercial burglary, grand theft, and auto theft patterns across all nine divisions.

Special operations bureau

Specialized units

SWAT

Special weapons & tactics

High-risk warrant service, armed barricade response, hostage rescue, and dignitary protection. 72-officer unit with full tactical equipment complement.

GND

Gang & narcotics division

Intelligence-led operations targeting gang infrastructure and narcotics distribution. Operates uniformed, plainclothes, and deep-cover units.

Vice

Vice unit

Enforces laws related to organized prostitution, illegal gambling operations, and related criminal enterprises across the city.

Air support

Air support division

Fleet of nine helicopters and four fixed-wing aircraft providing surveillance, pursuit support, and search and rescue over Los Santos.

K-9

K-9 unit

Thirty-two trained canine teams for narcotics detection, tracking, building searches, and officer protection across all divisions.

Motor

Motor unit

Motorcycle officers assigned to traffic enforcement, special events, dignitary escort, and high-visibility patrol on major corridors.

EOD

Explosive ordnance disposal

Bomb squad response to explosive threats, suspicious packages, and post-blast investigation across the city and LSIA.

SIS

Special investigation section

Long-term surveillance and investigation of major organized crime figures operating within Los Santos.

Academy Class 118

About the academy

The LSPD is accepting applications for Academy Class 118. Training begins September 8, 2026 at the LSPD Training Academy, Elysian Island.

The academy runs 22 weeks and covers use of force law, firearms qualification, emergency vehicle operations, report writing, community policing, first aid, defensive tactics, and a field training rotation in a live division. Recruits are assigned as Police Officer I upon graduation with full city benefits.
